A LiteLLM vulnerability hit and I'd been proposing infrastructure to scan for VS Code extensions fleet-wide — a blind spot in our coverage. Leadership was eager to move fast, and I shared that urgency, but the scope was real: 2,700 devices across desktop engineering's fleet, requiring Jamf scripts, policies, extension attributes, and API hookups. I wrote up a staged plan — local testing first, then a small device group, then expanding rollouts each day with full logging at every step — and walked leadership through why each phase mattered. It took about a week. The approach worked because everyone understood the tradeoffs upfront: we moved as fast as we safely could, with clear checkpoints so leadership always knew where things stood. That kind of transparency — here's where we are, here's why, here's what's next — is how I keep momentum without cutting corners.
