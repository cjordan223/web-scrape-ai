My first week at Great Wolf Resorts, I was assigned to unify security dashboards in Rapid7 — pulling in CrowdStrike, FireEye, and Microsoft Defender data. I had no idea how to approach it. I went to one of the stronger engineers on the team for guidance, and when my manager got excited about the direction, I told him it was my coworker's idea. Protecting that working relationship mattered more than getting credit — what I could learn from that person was worth far more than kudos on one project. That instinct has stayed with me. At both GWR and UCOP, I've built systems that other engineers need to inherit. Six months after leaving GWR, my old boss texted me a credentials question — which told me the automation was still running fine without me. I take handoff seriously because I've been the person trying to figure out someone else's undocumented system.
