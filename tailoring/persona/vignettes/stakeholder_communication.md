At UCOP, I work with a long leash and non-technical leadership. That means when I check in after days of deep work with 15,000 new lines in the codebase, I have maybe 15 minutes to explain what changed and why it matters. I've gotten good at that translation: this is the problem, this is why I'm solving it this way, this is how we safeguard against it. When leadership wanted to scan for VS Code extensions fleet-wide across 2,700 devices, I put together a staged rollout plan and walked through the timeline so we were aligned on scope and safety before starting. The UC system has a triple-tiered leadership structure where direction filters down in increasingly abstract form. A big part of the job is unpacking what the actual ask is. Rapport and transparency make that back-and-forth natural — I ask for clarity early rather than building the wrong thing quietly.
