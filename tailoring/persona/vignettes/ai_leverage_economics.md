AI tooling is a leverage point, and this is the bleeding edge of where security engineering is going. With development background plus AI-assisted workflows, a single security engineer can architect and deliver full-stack enterprise solutions to problems that previously required a dedicated engineering team or a six-figure SaaS license. Coraline is the clearest proof — an asset-correlation platform that would normally mean a CMDB integration project or a vendor contract, built and operated solo. That changes build-vs-buy math on internal tooling: problems that used to get deferred because headcount was not available now get solved in-house, on timelines that beat procurement cycles. This is the operating model for modern security engineering — lean teams producing output at the scale of much larger orgs by treating AI as force multiplier, not autopilot. Orgs that internalize this early compound the advantage; the ones that wait end up paying vendor prices for problems their own engineers could have solved.
