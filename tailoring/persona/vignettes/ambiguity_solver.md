I gravitate toward problems where people know something is broken but can't even quantify what's causing it, let alone how to fix it. At UCOP, that was the pattern — someone would surface a pain point, and my job was to figure out if it was a tooling gap, a data problem, or an organizational blind spot, then build the solution. Coraline started that way: "which assets are missing vulnerability agents?" turned into a full-stack correlation platform once I dug into the data. TexTailor started that way too — a personal itch about job search friction that became a multi-service pipeline. The research phase is genuinely rewarding. I'll encounter tools or techniques I didn't know existed, and that feeds the next problem. The harder part is usually not solving the problem but explaining the solution — when the answer wasn't obvious, the explanation rarely is either. So I end up telling the story of the investigation as much as presenting the fix.
