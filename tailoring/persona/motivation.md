## What drives me
I like problems where people know something is broken but have no idea how to even frame the solution. The research phase — digging into docs, discovering tech I didn't know existed, learning a new facet of a tool — is genuinely rewarding, not a chore. I don't see ambiguous problems as hassles; I see them as puzzles that I know can be solved.

## What I'm looking for
Small teams with clear goals, fast-moving, where people are genuinely enthusiastic about the technology. I want an environment with real peer feedback — engineers who think like me and engineers who don't, pushing each other to get better. I've spent a lot of time setting the tempo alone; I want to be around people who raise mine.

## What frustrates me about the field
Security gets treated as IT-adjacent — patching apps, resetting passwords, running compliance checklists. Meanwhile, harder-to-quantify but real threats get deprioritized in favor of security theater that looks good in management meetings but has more holes than Swiss cheese in practice. I want to work somewhere that takes the actual threat landscape as seriously as the audit trail.

## What I build for fun
I tinker constantly — home lab, locally-hosted SaaS apps, AI tools, this entire job search platform (TexTailor). I use AI coding tools daily in personal projects, pushing them harder than most production environments would allow, specifically so I can understand where they break. Breaking things at home gave me enough hands-on context to build governance frameworks at work.
